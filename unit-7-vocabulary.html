Core Technical Vocabulary

Say each term aloud, locate it on a diagram or in the engine room, and use it in a complete sentence. Avoid learning isolated translations only.

TermPlain-English meaningOnboard example
1. blackouttotal loss of electrical powerThe vessel experienced a blackout during maneuvering.
2. emergency generatorindependent generator supplying essential servicesThe emergency generator started automatically.
3. machinery-space firefire located in an engine or machinery spaceThe fixed fire-extinguishing system may be required.
4. quick-closing valveremotely operated valve shutting fuel supplyClose the quick-closing valves from the emergency station.
5. remote stopdevice stopping machinery from outside the spaceActivate the ventilation remote stop.
6. floodinguncontrolled entry of waterFlooding was reported in the shaft tunnel.
7. bilge levelheight of liquid collected in the bilgeThe bilge level is rising rapidly.
8. oil spillrelease of oil into the ship or environmentThe oil spill was contained with absorbent booms.
9. SOPEPshipboard oil pollution emergency planFollow the SOPEP reporting and response procedures.
10. containmentaction preventing a substance from spreadingContainment was established around the leaking flange.
11. emergency teamdesignated group responding to an emergencyThe emergency team assembled with breathing apparatus.
12. status updatebrief report of current condition and actionsSend the bridge a status update every five minutes.

Pronunciation and Number Practice

  • Stress the important content words: equipment name, number, location, value, and action.
  • Separate similar terms clearly: pressure / temperature, suction / discharge, alarm / trip.
  • Read decimals digit by digit when clarity is critical: 4.5 bar = four point five bar.
  • Read equipment numbers consistently: Generator No. 2, No. 4 cylinder, tank one port.

Vocabulary Production Drill

  1. Choose five terms and explain each without using the term itself.
  2. Write one normal-condition sentence and one abnormal-condition sentence.
  3. Give a partner an instruction containing an equipment name, location, and action.
  4. Report a reading with the correct unit and state whether it is stable, rising, or falling.
