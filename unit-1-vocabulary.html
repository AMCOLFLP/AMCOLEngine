Core Technical Vocabulary

Say each term aloud, locate it on a diagram or in the engine room, and use it in a complete sentence. Avoid learning isolated translations only.

TermPlain-English meaningOnboard example
1. engine roomthe machinery space containing propulsion and auxiliary systemsThe duty engineer entered the engine room through the main access door.
2. control roomthe enclosed space used to monitor and control machineryThe alarm was acknowledged from the engine control room.
3. escape routea marked path used to leave a dangerous spaceKeep the escape route free from tools and hoses.
4. hot surfacea component that can cause burnsA warning sign was fitted near the hot exhaust manifold.
5. rotating machineryequipment with moving shafts, couplings, or bladesDo not wear loose clothing near rotating machinery.
6. oil leakageunwanted escape of oil from a pipe, seal, or machineReport any oil leakage immediately.
7. hearing protectionearplugs or earmuffs used in high-noise areasHearing protection is required on the lower platform.
8. safety goggleseye protection against particles and splashesWear safety goggles before opening the chemical container.
9. permit to workformal authorization for a hazardous jobA permit to work is required for hot work.
10. lockout/tagoutisolation procedure preventing accidental energizingApply lockout/tagout before maintenance.
11. toolbox meetingshort safety briefing before a jobThe second engineer conducted a toolbox meeting.
12. near missan event that could have caused injury or damageThe near miss was recorded and discussed.

Pronunciation and Number Practice

  • Stress the important content words: equipment name, number, location, value, and action.
  • Separate similar terms clearly: pressure / temperature, suction / discharge, alarm / trip.
  • Read decimals digit by digit when clarity is critical: 4.5 bar = four point five bar.
  • Read equipment numbers consistently: Generator No. 2, No. 4 cylinder, tank one port.

Vocabulary Production Drill

  1. Choose five terms and explain each without using the term itself.
  2. Write one normal-condition sentence and one abnormal-condition sentence.
  3. Give a partner an instruction containing an equipment name, location, and action.
  4. Report a reading with the correct unit and state whether it is stable, rising, or falling.
