Core Technical Vocabulary

Say each term aloud, locate it on a diagram or in the engine room, and use it in a complete sentence. Avoid learning isolated translations only.

TermPlain-English meaningOnboard example
1. cylinder linerreplaceable cylindrical surface in which the piston movesThe cylinder liner is inspected for wear and scuffing.
2. piston crownupper part of the piston exposed to combustionCooling oil circulates through the piston crown.
3. piston ringsrings that seal gas and control lubricating oilThe piston rings prevent combustion gas leakage.
4. connecting rodlink transmitting force from piston to crankshaftThe connecting rod transfers reciprocating motion.
5. crossheadsliding joint connecting piston rod and connecting rod in a large two-stroke engineThe crosshead guides the piston rod vertically.
6. crankshaftshaft converting reciprocating motion into rotationThe crankshaft drives the propeller shaft.
7. camshaftshaft controlling valve or fuel-pump timingThe camshaft operates the exhaust valve mechanism.
8. exhaust valvevalve releasing combustion gasesThe exhaust valve opens near the end of the power stroke.
9. fuel injectordevice spraying atomized fuel into the cylinderThe fuel injector must produce a fine spray pattern.
10. turbochargerunit using exhaust gas to compress scavenge airThe turbocharger increases the mass of air supplied.
11. scavenge receiverair manifold supplying pressurized air to cylindersScavenge air enters through ports in the liner.
12. main bearingbearing supporting the crankshaftMain-bearing temperature is monitored continuously.

Pronunciation and Number Practice

  • Stress the important content words: equipment name, number, location, value, and action.
  • Separate similar terms clearly: pressure / temperature, suction / discharge, alarm / trip.
  • Read decimals digit by digit when clarity is critical: 4.5 bar = four point five bar.
  • Read equipment numbers consistently: Generator No. 2, No. 4 cylinder, tank one port.

Vocabulary Production Drill

  1. Choose five terms and explain each without using the term itself.
  2. Write one normal-condition sentence and one abnormal-condition sentence.
  3. Give a partner an instruction containing an equipment name, location, and action.
  4. Report a reading with the correct unit and state whether it is stable, rising, or falling.
