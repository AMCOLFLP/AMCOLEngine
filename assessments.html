Combined Assessments: Diagnostic Pre-Test and Comprehensive Post-Test

Part 1: Diagnostic Pre-Test

Complete this test before Unit 1. It identifies present knowledge of engine-room vocabulary, grammar, safety communication, watchkeeping, maintenance, and emergency reporting.

1. Which sentence is the clearest safety instruction?

2. Which term means the pipe through which fluid enters a pump?

3. Which sentence correctly explains function?

4. Which tense is best for an event completed at 0215?

5. What should be included in a watch handover?

6. What is the safest response to an unclear valve instruction?

7. Which sentence separates observation from cause?

8. What must happen before closing a generator breaker?

9. Which report is most complete?

10. What completes a closed-loop instruction?

Short Diagnostic Writing

Write 80–120 words describing an abnormal pump condition. Include equipment identity, location, reading, observation, immediate action, and confirmation.

Part 2: Comprehensive Post-Test

The student post-test unlocks after all seven units are marked complete.

1. Which sentence is the clearest safety instruction?

2. Which term means the pipe through which fluid enters a pump?

3. Which sentence correctly explains function?

4. Which tense is best for an event completed at 0215?

5. What should be included in a watch handover?

6. What is the safest response to an unclear valve instruction?

7. Which sentence separates observation from cause?

8. What must happen before closing a generator breaker?

9. Which report is most complete?

10. What completes a closed-loop instruction?

11. Which logbook sentence is correct?

12. Which conditional is correct?

13. What is the first priority in a machinery-space fire report?

14. Which phrase reports a trend?

15. Which maintenance closing report is complete?

Integrated Writing Task

Write a 150–200 word engine-room incident report about a generator alarm followed by a controlled changeover. Include time, readings, actions, communication with the bridge, and final status.

Speaking Performance

Complete a three-minute role-play involving an instruction, read-back, abnormal-condition report, corrective action, and completion report.
