Teacher Guide, Answer Keys, and Rubrics

2. Teaching Approach

  1. Show the equipment or diagram before introducing the term.
  2. Model the phrase in a complete operational sentence.
  3. Require a read-back for all critical instructions.
  4. Ask students to report observations before giving possible causes.
  5. Move from controlled practice to timed role-play.
  6. Finish with a written logbook or maintenance entry.

5. Interactive Answer Key

The “Show answers” button reveals vocabulary and multiple-choice answers on each unit’s Practice & Check page. Teachers should require students to explain why an answer is correct rather than only copying it.

6. Remediation Priorities

  • Replace vague words with equipment identity and location.
  • Practise numbers, decimals, units, and time.
  • Separate present status, completed event, and future action.
  • Use read-back and completion reporting until automatic.
  • Correct unsafe language immediately: unclear valve identity, missing isolation, premature reset, or unsupported diagnosis.
