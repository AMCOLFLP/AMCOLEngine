Core Technical Vocabulary

Say each term aloud, locate it on a diagram or in the engine room, and use it in a complete sentence. Avoid learning isolated translations only.

TermPlain-English meaningOnboard example
1. overhaulcomplete disassembly, inspection, repair, and reassemblyThe fuel pump is scheduled for overhaul.
2. inspectioncareful examination of conditionVisual inspection revealed a cracked gasket.
3. bearingcomponent supporting and guiding a rotating shaftThe bearing clearance was measured.
4. gasketsealing material placed between joint facesReplace the damaged gasket before reassembly.
5. clearancesmall designed gap between componentsMeasure the valve clearance with a feeler gauge.
6. torque wrenchtool applying a specified tightening torqueUse a calibrated torque wrench on the cylinder-cover nuts.
7. pullertool used to remove a tightly fitted componentA hydraulic puller was used to remove the coupling.
8. spare partreplacement component kept for maintenanceConfirm that the correct spare part is available.
9. weargradual loss of material through use or frictionThe shaft sleeve shows excessive wear.
10. misalignmentincorrect position of connected shafts or partsMisalignment can cause vibration and seal damage.
11. troubleshootingsystematic process of finding a faultTroubleshooting began with the power supply.
12. corrective actionaction taken to remove a defect or causeThe corrective action was to renew the seal.

Pronunciation and Number Practice

  • Stress the important content words: equipment name, number, location, value, and action.
  • Separate similar terms clearly: pressure / temperature, suction / discharge, alarm / trip.
  • Read decimals digit by digit when clarity is critical: 4.5 bar = four point five bar.
  • Read equipment numbers consistently: Generator No. 2, No. 4 cylinder, tank one port.

Vocabulary Production Drill

  1. Choose five terms and explain each without using the term itself.
  2. Write one normal-condition sentence and one abnormal-condition sentence.
  3. Give a partner an instruction containing an equipment name, location, and action.
  4. Report a reading with the correct unit and state whether it is stable, rising, or falling.
