Conversation Script

Chief Engineer: Show me the components that transmit power from the piston to the propeller.

Cadet: The piston force is transmitted through the piston rod, crosshead, connecting rod, and crankshaft.

Chief Engineer: What is the function of the crosshead?

Cadet: It guides the piston rod and transfers force to the connecting rod while reducing side thrust on the liner.

Chief Engineer: Where is the main bearing located?

Cadet: It is located in the bedplate and supports the crankshaft.

Chief Engineer: What defect can occur at the cylinder liner?

Cadet: Wear, scuffing, cracking, or excessive lubrication can occur.

Chief Engineer: Good. Report any abnormal temperature or noise immediately.

Cadet: Understood. I will monitor bearing temperatures and crankcase sounds.

Listening Comprehension

  1. What equipment or system is being discussed?
  2. Which readings, positions, defects, or hazards are reported?
  3. Which line shows a read-back or confirmation?
  4. What action is required before the operation can continue?
  5. What final result must be reported or recorded?

Pronunciation and Delivery

  • Pause after the equipment name and before the reading.
  • Stress numbers, units, directions, valve positions, and negative words.
  • Use a steady speed. Do not rush a safety-critical message.
  • End a report with the required next action or confirmation.
  • Repeat any unclear number using individual digits.

Role-Play Extension

Change one detail in the script: equipment number, reading, defect, or required action. The partner must repeat the changed detail accurately before responding.
