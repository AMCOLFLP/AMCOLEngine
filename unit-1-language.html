UNIT 1 · TECHNICAL LANGUAGE

Technical Language

Standard phrases for safe, concise, and closed-loop engine-room communication.

Core Communication Patterns

FunctionModel phrase
Giving a safety instructionWear your hearing protection before entering the lower platform.
Checking understandingRepeat the safety instruction, please.
Reporting a hazardThere is oil on the deck near the purifier.
Stopping unsafe workStop the job. The pump has not been isolated.
Confirming actionUnderstood. I will isolate the electrical supply now.
Closing the loopIsolation completed and tag fitted.

The Closed-Loop Pattern

1. Sender: gives a clear instruction with equipment identity and action.

2. Receiver: repeats the critical details.

3. Sender: confirms or corrects the read-back.

4. Receiver: performs the action and reports completion and result.

Example: “Start generator No. 3 and report voltage.” — “Start generator No. 3 and report voltage.” — “Correct.” — “Generator started; voltage 440 volts and stable.”

Say This, Not That

Avoid vague languageUse precise technical language
The thing is bad.The No. 2 pump discharge pressure is low.
It is over there.The leakage is at the forward flange on the starboard side.
I think maybe the bearing.A knocking sound is coming from the drive-end bearing area.
Okay.Understood. I will close valve F-12 now.
Finished.Valve F-12 closed and position indicator checked.
Temperature high.No. 4 cylinder exhaust temperature is 465 degrees and rising.

Message-Building Formula

Equipment + location + condition/reading + action + confirmation

“Jacket-water pump No. 2, lower platform: discharge pressure is 2.1 bar and falling. I am changing over to the standby pump. I will report when pressure is restored.”
