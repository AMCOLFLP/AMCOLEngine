Grammar

Use grammar as an operational tool for accurate instructions, explanations, reports, and records.

Countable and uncountable nouns, quantities, measurements, and passive instructions

In marine engineering communication, grammar shows time, responsibility, certainty, obligation, sequence, and cause. A small grammar error can change whether an action is completed, required, recommended, or still in progress.

Communication purposePatternEngineering example
Countablea valve / two valves / several pumpsClose the two cross-connection valves.
Uncountableoil, water, air, pressure, equipmentThere is some oil in the drain tank.
Quantitymuch/many, little/few, amount/numberHow much fuel remains? How many valves are open?
Measurementnumber + unitPressure is 4.5 bar. Temperature is 78 degrees Celsius.
Passive procedureis opened / must be closedThe suction valve must be opened before the pump is started.

Common Errors and Corrections

AvoidUse this form
There are many oil in the tank.There is a large amount of oil in the tank.
The pressure are five bar.The pressure is five bar.
Open valve number two before started pump.Open valve number two before starting the pump.
The pump has a cavitations.The pump is cavitating / The pump has cavitation.
Correction habit: First identify the intended time or function. Then choose the correct verb form. Finally check the equipment number, unit, and action.

Guided Grammar Practice

  1. Rewrite one instruction as a prohibition.
  2. Rewrite one active sentence in passive voice.
  3. Change a completed event with a stated time into past simple.
  4. Write one sentence describing a current action and one describing a normal routine.
  5. Write a cause-and-effect sentence about an abnormal machinery condition.

Engineering Sentence Checklist

  • Is the subject or equipment clearly identified?
  • Does the verb show the correct time or status?
  • Is the instruction direct and measurable?
  • Are the number and unit included where needed?
  • Does the sentence separate fact from assumption?
