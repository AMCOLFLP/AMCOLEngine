Core Technical Vocabulary

Say each term aloud, locate it on a diagram or in the engine room, and use it in a complete sentence. Avoid learning isolated translations only.

TermPlain-English meaningOnboard example
1. watchkeepingcontinuous monitoring and operation during an assigned periodThe engineer is responsible for watchkeeping from 0000 to 0400.
2. handoverformal transfer of responsibility and informationThe handover included all ongoing defects.
3. standing orderpermanent instruction issued by the chief engineerRead the chief engineer’s standing orders.
4. night orderspecific instruction for the overnight periodThe night order required hourly bearing checks.
5. machinery readingmeasured value from an instrumentRecord each machinery reading accurately.
6. trenddirection in which a value changes over timeThe exhaust temperature shows an upward trend.
7. alarm acknowledgementconfirmation that an alarm has been noticedAlarm acknowledgement does not mean the fault is corrected.
8. bypasstemporary disabling or diversion of a protective functionThe alarm bypass must be reported at handover.
9. logbook entryformal written record of an event or conditionMake a logbook entry after changing over pumps.
10. roundssystematic inspection of machinery spacesComplete engine-room rounds every hour.
11. abnormal conditiona value, sound, smell, or behavior outside normal limitsReport any abnormal condition immediately.
12. UMSunattended machinery space operating modeThe vessel changed to UMS mode at 2200.

Pronunciation and Number Practice

  • Stress the important content words: equipment name, number, location, value, and action.
  • Separate similar terms clearly: pressure / temperature, suction / discharge, alarm / trip.
  • Read decimals digit by digit when clarity is critical: 4.5 bar = four point five bar.
  • Read equipment numbers consistently: Generator No. 2, No. 4 cylinder, tank one port.

Vocabulary Production Drill

  1. Choose five terms and explain each without using the term itself.
  2. Write one normal-condition sentence and one abnormal-condition sentence.
  3. Give a partner an instruction containing an equipment name, location, and action.
  4. Report a reading with the correct unit and state whether it is stable, rising, or falling.
