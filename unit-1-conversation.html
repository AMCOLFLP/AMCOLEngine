Conversation Script

Second Engineer: Before we start, identify the hazards around the fuel-oil purifier.

Engine Cadet: There is a hot surface, rotating machinery, and a possible oil-splash hazard.

Second Engineer: Correct. What personal protective equipment is required?

Engine Cadet: Helmet, safety shoes, coveralls, gloves, goggles, and hearing protection.

Second Engineer: Good. Is the purifier electrically isolated?

Engine Cadet: Not yet. I will isolate the power supply and fit the warning tag.

Second Engineer: Do not begin until I verify the isolation.

Engine Cadet: Understood. Work will not begin until the isolation is verified.

Second Engineer: Report back when the area is safe.

Engine Cadet: Isolation verified, spill tray positioned, and escape route clear.

Listening Comprehension

  1. What equipment or system is being discussed?
  2. Which readings, positions, defects, or hazards are reported?
  3. Which line shows a read-back or confirmation?
  4. What action is required before the operation can continue?
  5. What final result must be reported or recorded?

Pronunciation and Delivery

  • Pause after the equipment name and before the reading.
  • Stress numbers, units, directions, valve positions, and negative words.
  • Use a steady speed. Do not rush a safety-critical message.
  • End a report with the required next action or confirmation.
  • Repeat any unclear number using individual digits.

Role-Play Extension

Change one detail in the script: equipment number, reading, defect, or required action. The partner must repeat the changed detail accurately before responding.
