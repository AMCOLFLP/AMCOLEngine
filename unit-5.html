Expanded Main Discussion: Why this unit matters

Maintenance communication must clearly distinguish what was observed, what was done, what remains to be done, and whether equipment is safe to return to service.

The language in this unit is organized around a practical cycle: observe → identify → communicate → act → confirm → record. Students should not only memorize words. They should use the words in complete operational messages with locations, measurements, equipment identity, and required action.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this unit, students should be able to:

  • Name common hand tools, measuring tools, consumables, and spare parts.
  • Describe planned and completed maintenance using correct tense.
  • Report symptoms, possible causes, and recommended actions separately.
  • Give advice and obligations with must, should, and have to.
  • Write a concise defect and maintenance report.
Engine-room standard: State the equipment, location, condition, action, and confirmation. Avoid vague words such as “thing,” “there,” “bad,” or “maybe” when precise information is available.

Operational Communication Framework

StageCommunication requirement
1. ObserveWhat do you see, hear, smell, or measure?
2. IdentifyWhich machine, component, tank, valve, or location?
3. ReportState the fact, reading, trend, or hazard clearly.
4. ActGive or follow the approved instruction.
5. ConfirmRepeat critical details and report completion.
6. RecordWrite the event, action, and result in the correct record.
