Conversation Script

Outgoing Engineer: Main engine is at 72 rpm. All pressures are normal.

Incoming Engineer: Main engine at 72 rpm, pressures normal. Any defects or alarms?

Outgoing Engineer: Generator No. 1 is under maintenance. Generator No. 2 is on load; No. 3 is standby.

Incoming Engineer: Generator No. 1 unavailable, No. 2 on load, No. 3 standby.

Outgoing Engineer: The jacket-water temperature on No. 4 cylinder has risen from 78 to 84 degrees.

Incoming Engineer: Rising jacket-water temperature on No. 4 cylinder. Has the chief engineer been informed?

Outgoing Engineer: Yes. Continue monitoring every fifteen minutes and call him at 88 degrees.

Incoming Engineer: Monitor every fifteen minutes and call at 88 degrees. Understood.

Outgoing Engineer: One bilge alarm channel is bypassed for testing. It is recorded in the logbook.

Incoming Engineer: Bypass noted and logbook checked. I accept the watch.

Listening Comprehension

  1. What equipment or system is being discussed?
  2. Which readings, positions, defects, or hazards are reported?
  3. Which line shows a read-back or confirmation?
  4. What action is required before the operation can continue?
  5. What final result must be reported or recorded?

Pronunciation and Delivery

  • Pause after the equipment name and before the reading.
  • Stress numbers, units, directions, valve positions, and negative words.
  • Use a steady speed. Do not rush a safety-critical message.
  • End a report with the required next action or confirmation.
  • Repeat any unclear number using individual digits.

Role-Play Extension

Change one detail in the script: equipment number, reading, defect, or required action. The partner must repeat the changed detail accurately before responding.
