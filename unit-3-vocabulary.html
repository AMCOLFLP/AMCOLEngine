Core Technical Vocabulary

Say each term aloud, locate it on a diagram or in the engine room, and use it in a complete sentence. Avoid learning isolated translations only.

TermPlain-English meaningOnboard example
1. centrifugal pumpdynamic pump using a rotating impellerThe centrifugal pump supplies cooling water.
2. gear pumppositive-displacement pump using meshing gearsA gear pump is suitable for viscous fuel oil.
3. suction linepipe through which fluid enters a pumpCheck that the suction line is open.
4. discharge linepipe through which fluid leaves a pumpThe discharge line leads to the settling tank.
5. non-return valvevalve allowing flow in one direction onlyThe non-return valve prevents backflow.
6. relief valvevalve protecting a system against excessive pressureThe relief valve lifts at the set pressure.
7. strainerdevice removing solid particles from fluidClean the sea-water strainer before starting the pump.
8. primingremoving air and filling a pump with liquidThe pump requires priming before startup.
9. cavitationformation and collapse of vapor bubbles in a pumpCavitation can cause noise, vibration, and impeller damage.
10. flow ratevolume of fluid moved per unit timeThe flow rate is 45 cubic metres per hour.
11. tank soundingmeasurement of liquid depth in a tankTake a tank sounding before transfer.
12. line-uparrangement of valves for a specific operationVerify the ballast-system line-up.

Pronunciation and Number Practice

  • Stress the important content words: equipment name, number, location, value, and action.
  • Separate similar terms clearly: pressure / temperature, suction / discharge, alarm / trip.
  • Read decimals digit by digit when clarity is critical: 4.5 bar = four point five bar.
  • Read equipment numbers consistently: Generator No. 2, No. 4 cylinder, tank one port.

Vocabulary Production Drill

  1. Choose five terms and explain each without using the term itself.
  2. Write one normal-condition sentence and one abnormal-condition sentence.
  3. Give a partner an instruction containing an equipment name, location, and action.
  4. Report a reading with the correct unit and state whether it is stable, rising, or falling.
