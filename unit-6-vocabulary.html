Core Technical Vocabulary

Say each term aloud, locate it on a diagram or in the engine room, and use it in a complete sentence. Avoid learning isolated translations only.

TermPlain-English meaningOnboard example
1. generatormachine converting mechanical energy into electrical energyGenerator No. 2 is supplying 440 volts.
2. main switchboardpanel distributing electrical powerThe main switchboard is located in the control room.
3. circuit breakerdevice opening or closing an electrical circuitThe circuit breaker tripped on overcurrent.
4. busbarconductor distributing high electrical currentThe busbar must be isolated before inspection.
5. synchronizationmatching voltage, frequency, and phase before connectionCheck synchronization before closing the breaker.
6. load sharingdistribution of electrical load between generatorsThe generators are operating in automatic load sharing.
7. sensordevice detecting a physical conditionThe temperature sensor sends a signal to the controller.
8. transmitterdevice converting a measurement into a standard signalThe pressure transmitter output is unstable.
9. interlockprotective logic preventing an unsafe actionThe interlock prevents the breaker from closing.
10. alarmwarning that an abnormal condition existsA low insulation alarm was displayed.
11. tripautomatic shutdown to protect equipmentThe motor tripped on overload.
12. resetrestoration of a protective device after fault clearanceDo not reset the trip until the cause is confirmed.

Pronunciation and Number Practice

  • Stress the important content words: equipment name, number, location, value, and action.
  • Separate similar terms clearly: pressure / temperature, suction / discharge, alarm / trip.
  • Read decimals digit by digit when clarity is critical: 4.5 bar = four point five bar.
  • Read equipment numbers consistently: Generator No. 2, No. 4 cylinder, tank one port.

Vocabulary Production Drill

  1. Choose five terms and explain each without using the term itself.
  2. Write one normal-condition sentence and one abnormal-condition sentence.
  3. Give a partner an instruction containing an equipment name, location, and action.
  4. Report a reading with the correct unit and state whether it is stable, rising, or falling.
