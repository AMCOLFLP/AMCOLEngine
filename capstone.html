Final Capstone Simulation

Scenario: From Normal Watch to Machinery Emergency

You are part of the 1200–1600 engine-room watch. The vessel is approaching port. Generator No. 2 is on load, generator No. 3 is standby, and the main engine is on maneuvering readiness. During rounds, the cadet reports increasing jacket-water temperature on No. 4 cylinder, a small oil leak near a transfer pump, and an intermittent alarm from the main switchboard. The bridge then orders standby engine. Ten minutes later, generator No. 2 trips and smoke is observed near its terminal box.

Student Tasks

  1. Conduct the watch handover.
  2. Report the three abnormal observations with exact location and readings.
  3. Give and read back pump-isolation instructions.
  4. Prepare and synchronize the standby generator.
  5. Respond to the trip and smoke report.
  6. Coordinate propulsion and power status with the bridge.
  7. Write a chronological logbook entry and a short defect report.

Required Evidence

EvidenceRequirement
Spoken communication8–10 minute team simulation with closed-loop exchanges.
LogbookTime, event, reading, action, officer informed, result.
Defect reportEquipment, symptom, checks, probable cause stated as provisional, recommendation.
Reflection100 words: what language prevented misunderstanding?
